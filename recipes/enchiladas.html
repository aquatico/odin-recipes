Enchiladas

Mexican enchiladas are a traditional dish that consist of a corn tortilla filled with a variety of ingredients, such as meat, cheese, or beans, and then rolled and covered in a chili pepper sauce. The dish is typically served with toppings such as sour cream, cheese, and diced onions. Enchiladas can be filled with a variety of meats including chicken, beef, pork or can be vegetarian. The sauce used to cover the enchiladas can vary depending on the region, with some using a red sauce made from dried chili peppers and others using a green sauce made from tomatillos.

Enchiladas are a popular dish in Mexico, and can be found in many Mexican restaurants and homes. They are often served as a main course, but can also be found as a side dish. The dish has also been adopted and adapted by Tex-Mex cuisine, where it is often served with a side of rice and beans. Enchiladas are a versatile dish that can be made in many different ways, allowing for endless flavor combinations and variations. They can be made with different types of fillings, sauces, and toppings, making them a favorite for many people.

Ingredients

  • 12 corn tortillas
  • 1 pound of shredded chicken, beef, pork or cheese
  • 1 cup of enchilada sauce (red or green)
  • 1 cup of shredded cheese
  • Optional toppings: sour cream, diced onions, cilantro, sliced jalapenos

Instructions

  1. Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. Heat the tortillas in a skillet or on a comal to make them more pliable.
  3. In a large bowl, mix together the shredded meat or cheese and 1/2 cup of the enchilada sauce.
  4. Place a spoonful of the meat mixture onto each tortilla and roll it up.
  5. Place the rolled enchiladas seam side down in a baking dish.
  6. Pour the remaining enchilada sauce over the enchiladas and top with shredded cheese.
  7. Bake in the preheated oven for 15-20 minutes, or until the cheese is melted and bubbly.
  8. Serve the enchiladas with your desired toppings and enjoy!
